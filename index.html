Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of digital technology and human behavior. Particularly, I study heterophily (the tendency to develop diverse networks, e.g., friendship, acquaintanceship), diversity, and identity on social media utilizing computational social science approach. Currently, I am working on these following projects.

Heterophily on Social Networking Sites. Advised by Professor Todd L. Pittinsky, Ph.D., I examine heterophily concerning race, ideology, and faith on social networking sites such as Facebook. I aim to answer, are developing interracial and interideological friendships amid the increasing polarization in our society still possible? If so, under what conditions are individuals willing to befriend those who are different?

Racial Identity on Social Media. Collaborating with Professor Jason J. Jones, Ph.D., I study how people identify themselves as members of particular race or ethnicity on social media, especially Twitter. Even though this social identity is salient in face-to-face interaction, it is not always the case with the online world. Hence, I am intrigued to learn why some people deliberately put racial identifiers (e.g., black, Asian) on their bios while others avoid it. Does this choice have something to do with real world events at the given time?

Waste Management, Technology, and Diversity. Working under Professor David J. Tonjes, Ph.D. and Professor Elizabeth L. Hewitt, Ph.D., I investigate ways in which solid waste can be well managed utilizing new technologies, e.g., recycling. Linking this topic with diversity, I am curious to examine if and how diverse communities produce diverse waste.

I have also been involved in various voluntary work and community services such as an IT support and facilitator at Worker's Educational Association (WEA) Bristol, a tutor at Kelas Inpirasi - Indonesia Mengajar in Jakarta, a mentor at Sagasitas Indonesia, and an iniator of a cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) program to reduce anxiety among inmates at Sleman Correctional Institution, Special Region of Yogyakarta.
