Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of digital technology and human behavior. Particularly, I study heterophily, diversity, identity, and privacy on social media. Currently, I am working on these following projects.

Heterophily on Social Networking Sites. I examine the tendency to develop diverse networks or known as heterophily, concerning race and political ideology on social networking sites such as Facebook and Twitter. I aim to answer, “What models explain and predict individual’s tendency to develop diverse networks beyond a mere chance?”

Racial Identity on Social Media. I study how people identify themselves as members of particular race or ethnicity on social media, especially Twitter. Even though this social identity is salient in face-to-face interaction, it is not always the case with online world. Hence, I am intrigued to learn why some people deliberately put racial identifiers (e.g., black, Asian) on their bios while others avoid it.
